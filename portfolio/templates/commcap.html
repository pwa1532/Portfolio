Community Capture

Educ 602 - Videogames, Learning & School Design

Coure Overview
Videogames, Learning & School Design was an intersted class, housed in the school of education, that mainly dealt with how game mechanics can be applied to everyday life, specifically school. The main reading for the course was James Gee's "What Video Games Have to Teach Us about Learning and Literacy" but the class was basically "Reality is Broken" the course.

The Game
During the second half of the semester we broken into groups and designed games. After a couple of iterations, my group decided on a game called Community Capture. Community Capture is a augmented reality smartphone game played throughout a city by high schoool age students that aims at teaching them about their local community, government and culture. The game is a cross between risk and a scavenger hunt that involves teams of stuents traveling around their community attempting to capture locations by find its "capture point" and using either GPS or the camera to prove they were at the location. In order the find the capture point the team must travel to various "hints" that contain facts about the hints location with information about the capture point hidden within. These facts can come in the form of text, video, pictures, or any other form of media a smartphone supports. Teams must travel to the various locations on the map and interpret the hints in order to find the capture points. When a team captures the entire map (of which each teams has their own map and are not competing for the same lopcations, just time), they win.


The twist
In order to make the game a little more challenge the players, one person (a teacher or other non-player) acts as a bandit. This persons also travels around the community but instead of trying to capture locations, they try and steal them from other teams. When the bandit arrives at a location a notification is sent to the team that the bandit is trying to steal it. The team then has a change the thwart the by answer questions about the region. All the information they need to answer the questions was given in the hints and capture point but not directly. Teams must talk about what they learned and interpret the facts in order the answer the questions. If they fail to answer enough of the questions right, they keep the region, if not they must travel back to the location and recapture it.
