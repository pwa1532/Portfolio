SocialU

SI 682 - interactions and interfaces

Problem
Due to cultural differences, experience and backgrounds, many incoming students have a long period of adjustment in feeling comfortable in Ann Arbor and finding friends. We want to shorten this period and make it as painless as possible. We also want to encourage the sharing of cultures between locals and people from elsewhere. A complete overview of the project can be found at our project website.

Project Description
We plan on making a mobile application that will help connect students of all cultures through activities do to around Ann Arbor. Common interests will be the central idea around which people will connect. People that like to dance will be able to find other people that like to dance. This way even though cultures may be different, they will have a central idea around which to share and bond.

Interviews
In order to better understand the needs of our users, the SocialU team interviewed five individuals. They were asked questions about their time at the university, how they meet new friends, how they found out about activities they enjoyed attending, and if they were international students, how they developed their social networks when they first arrived. The results of this stage gave the team useful insights into how students, especailly international students, formed social networks around campus.

Personas & Scenarios
Now that the team had a better understanding of its users, they created a set of four personas to aid in their design of the site. Two of the personas represented local students that would interact with internatinal students and show them around town and two were international students who want to learn about their new environments and meet new people that share the same interests.


Lo-Fi & Hi-Fi Prototyes
The final steps of the process were to create a series of Lo-Fi and Hi-Fi prototypes. Both of these were created through of repeating cycles of design jams, discussion and refinement. After we had create a working set of Lo-Fi prototypes, the team should them to a series of potential users. This feed back was then used to inform and alter the designs before finally producing Hi-Fi protoypes. These again were shown to potential users and altered into the final product.
