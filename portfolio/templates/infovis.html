Email Visualization

SI 649 - Information Visualization

About the class
SI649 is designed to teach students about different aspects of visualizing information. Through a series of lectures, discussions and design exercises students learn all about the various static, dynamic and interactive methods people have used to visualize information and understand when, where and why they work.

Email visualization project
During the second half of the course, students are broken into small teams of three and build their own visualization. The topic my team choose to visualize is SI.ALL.OPEN, and opt-out open email list our graduate program uses that covers everything from notifying students there is free food to in 80 reply long threads about speeding tickets and gender pronouns (true story). My team wanted to better understand the makeup of the users of the list and what, if any, patterns were involved in its use. After spending some time discussing the system and researching other visualizations, my team decided to break the data along two dimensions, the groups who generated the messages (into students, administrators, and external sources) and general categories they fell into (food, spam, job posting, advice, etc). We then used a combination of a starburst and stacked bar graph to illustrate who post the message and all the replies.

After several round of prototyping and alterations, we finally ended up with two visualizations that varies in how we used color to represent data. The first used hues to encode the group each email belonged to with variants within a hue to dictate the subgroup it belonged to. The second used more diverse and therefore distinct colors only to separate subgroups but was easier to distinguish. We presented the visualizations to a series of students to determine if A) they could understand and use the visualization and B) if they did like the visualization, which of the two color schemes made more sense to them. The results were a smashing success with many fellow users enjoying using it and in fact getting very distracted exploring the data.

Since then
Though the initial version was a success, the short time span we had to create the system left the controls of the original display cluttered and cumbersome. As a result I have since modified the visualization with a slightly cleaner interface, though more work could be done.
